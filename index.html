Sentences


Convention/Grammar

Sentences: A sentence is a group of words that expresses a complete thought. It begins with a capital letter and ends with a punctuation mark.

Complete Sentences: A complete sentence has a subject and a predicate. It expresses a complete thought and makes sense on its own.

Fragment Sentences: A fragment sentence is an incomplete thought or idea. It lacks either a subject or a predicate, or both.

Run-on Sentences: A run-on sentence is two or more complete sentences joined together without proper punctuation. It does not have a clear stop or pause between independent clauses.
